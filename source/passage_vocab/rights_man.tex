\textbf{ridiculous} & adj. &  extremely silly or unreasonable \\
 & & \textit{She looks ridiculous in that outfit.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{whence} & adv. &  from where \\
 & & \textit{They returned to the land whence they came.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{precedent} & n. &  a similar action or event that happened at an earlier time \\
 & & \textit{There are no precedents for these events. [=nothing like these events has ever happened before]}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{administer} & v. &  to manage the operation of (something, such as a company or government) or the use of (something, such as property) \\
 & & \textit{As a cost-saving measure, voters have elected to have the two towns administered jointly.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{qualification} & n. &  a special skill or type of experience or knowledge that makes someone suitable to do a particular job or activity usually plural \\
 & & \textit{He has the best qualifications for the job.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{exhaust} & v. &  to use all of someone's mental or physical energy :to tire out or wear out (someone) completely \\
 & & \textit{If you keep working these long hours, you're just going to exhaust yourself. = Working these long hours will just exhaust you.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{null} & adj. &  having no legal power \\
 & & \textit{The contract was declared null. [=invalid]}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{tyranny} & n. &  cruel and unfair treatment by people with power over others \\
 & & \textit{The refugees were fleeing tyranny.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{fury} & n. &  violent anger :rage \\
 & & \textit{I could see the fury in her eyes.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{fashionable} & adj. &  currently popular \\
 & & \textit{fashionable clothes [=clothes that are popular; clothes that are in fashion]}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{pardon} & v. &  to officially say that someone who is guilty of a crime will be allowed to go free and will not be punished \\
 & & \textit{pardon a criminal}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{presumption} & n. &  a belief that something is true even though it has not been proved often + that \\
 & & \textit{The presumption is that the thief had a key to the store.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{dispose} & v. &  to cause (someone) to be likely to do or have something + to or toward \\
 & & \textit{His upbringing disposes him to question authority.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{reverence} & n. &  honor or respect that is felt for or shown to (someone or something) \\
 & & \textit{Her poems are treated with reverence by other poets.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{vanity} & n. &  the quality of people who have too much pride in their own appearance, abilities, achievements, etc. :the quality of being vain \\
 & & \textit{The handsome actor's vanity was well-known.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{contend} & v. &  to argue or state (something) in a strong and definite way \\
 & & \textit{These people contend that they have earned the right to the land.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{immortal} & adj. &  not capable of dying :living forever \\
 & & \textit{the immortal soul}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{cultivate} & v. &  to prepare and use (soil) for growing plants \\
 & & \textit{Prehistoric peoples settled the area and began to cultivate the land.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{repeal} & v. &  to officially make (a law) no longer valid \\
 & & \textit{The state legislature eventually repealed [=rescinded] the tax (law).}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{confine} & v. &  to keep (someone or something) within limits :to prevent (someone or something) from going beyond a particular limit, area, etc. usually + to \\
 & & \textit{Please confine [=restrict, limit] your comments to 200 words.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{awe} & v. &  to fill (someone) with awe \\
 & & \textit{Her style both awes and perplexes me.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{sans} & preposition &  without \\
 & & \textit{She went to the party sans her husband.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{instruct} & v. &  to teach (someone) a subject, skill, etc. usually + in or on \\
 & & \textit{His friend instructed him in English.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{preclude} & v. &  to make (something) impossible :to prevent (something) from happening \\
 & & \textit{She suffered an injury that precluded the possibility of an athletic career.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{thereon} & adv. &  on the thing that has been mentioned \\
 & & \textit{The highway and structures thereon are being repaired.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{dictate} & v. &  to speak or read (something) to a person who writes it down or to a machine that records it \\
 & & \textit{She's dictating a letter to her secretary.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{provoke} & v. &  to cause the occurrence of (a feeling or action) :to make (something) happen \\
 & & \textit{His remarks provoked both tears and laughter.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{marvelous} & adj. &  extremely good or enjoyable :wonderful \\
 & & \textit{We had a marvelous time at the party.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{monstrous} & adj. &  extremely or unusually large :gigantic \\
 & & \textit{a monstrous billboard}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{precede} & v. &  to happen, go, or come before (something or someone) \\
 & & \textit{Riots preceded the civil war.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{contradict} & v. &  to say the opposite of (something that someone else has said) :to deny the truth of (something) \\
 & & \textit{contradict a rumor}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{commence} & v. &  to begin \\
 & & \textit{The festivities will commence with a parade.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{posterity} & n. &  people in the future \\
 & & \textit{Posterity will remember her as a woman of courage and integrity.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{withhold} & v. &  to hold (something) back \\
 & & \textit{You can withhold the fee until the work is complete. [=you can wait to pay the fee until after the work is complete]}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{therefrom} & adv. &  from that or it\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{sectarian} & adj. &  relating to religious or political sects and the differences between them \\
 & & \textit{sectarian movements}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{formality} & n. &  a formal quality \\
 & & \textit{Her use of old-fashioned language lends an air of formality to her writing.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{grind} & v. &  to crush or break (something) into very small pieces by rubbing it against a rough surface or using a special machine \\
 & & \textit{Grind (up) the coffee beans into a fine powder.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{illuminate} & v. &  to supply (something) with light :to shine light on (something) \\
 & & \textit{Candles illuminate [=light] the church.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{voluminous} & adj. &  very large :containing a lot of space \\
 & & \textit{a voluminous room}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{genealogy} & n. &  the study of family history \\
 & & \textit{an expert in genealogy}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{frenzy} & n. &  great and often wild or uncontrolled activity \\
 & & \textit{the buying frenzy just before Christmas}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{despise} & v. &  to dislike (something or someone) very much \\
 & & \textit{He despises [=hates, loathes] pop music.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{infallible} & adj. &  not capable of being wrong or making mistakes :not fallible \\
 & & \textit{I never claimed to be infallible.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{inconvenient} & adj. &  causing trouble or problems :not convenient \\
 & & \textit{The restaurant is in an inconvenient location.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{explode} & v. &  to suddenly break apart in a violent way with parts flying outward \\
 & & \textit{The bomb could explode [=blow up, detonate] at any minute.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{copious} & adj. &  very large in amount or number \\
 & & \textit{The storm produced a copious amount of rain.}\\[0.08cm]

 & & \textit{omnipotent gods}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{horrid} & adj. &  very shocking or bad \\
 & & \textit{People there are living in horrid conditions.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{stag} & n. &  an adult male deer\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{turnpike} & n. &  a major road that you must pay to use \\
 & & \textit{the Massachusetts Turnpike}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{chasm} & n. &  a deep hole or opening in the surface of the earth :gorge\\
 & & \textit{Leaders tried to bridge a chasm [=split, divide] between the two religious groups.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{insolent} & adj. &  rude or impolite :having or showing a lack of respect for other people \\
 & & \textit{an insolent [=impudent, disrespectful] child}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{posse} & n. &  a group of people who were gathered together by a sheriff in the past to help search for a criminal \\
 & & \textit{The sheriff and his posse rode out to look for the bandits.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{annihilate} & v. &  to destroy (something or someone) completely \\
 & & \textit{Bombs annihilated the city.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{credulity} & n. &  ability or willingness to believe something \\
 & & \textit{Although most of the book is believable, its ending tests/strains credulity. [=its ending is difficult to believe; its ending does not seem true or possible]}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{irritate} & v. &  to make (someone) impatient, angry, or annoyed \\
 & & \textit{It's his arrogance that really irritates me.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{usurp} & v. &  to take and keep (something, such as power) in a forceful or violent way and especially without the right to do so \\
 & & \textit{Some people have accused city council members of trying to usurp the mayor's power.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{dissolute} & adj. &  sed to describe someone (such as a person who often gets drunk) whose way of living is considered morally wrong \\
 & & \textit{a dissolute drunk}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{upstart} & n. &  a person who has recently begun an activity, become successful, etc., and who does not show proper respect for older and more experienced people \\
 & & \textit{a young upstart from Harvard who thinks he knows more than the boss}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{congratulation} & n. &  a message telling someone that you are happy because of his or her success or good luck :words that congratulate someone \\
 & & \textit{Let me offer you my congratulations for/on being elected.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{unlettered} & adj. &  unable to read or write :illiterate \\
 & & \textit{unlettered peasants}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{nonentity} & n. &  a person who is not famous or important \\
 & & \textit{She quickly went from being a nonentity [=nobody] to being one of the most famous women in the country.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{consign} & v. &  to put (someone) in a usually unpleasant place or situation + to \\
 & & \textit{Survivors described how they were consigned to labor camps.}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{appertain} & v. &  to belong to or be connected or related to something :pertain \\
 & & \textit{the rights and privileges that appertain to marriage}\\[0.08cm]
\textbf{incivility} & n. &  a rude or impolite attitude or behavior :lack of civility \\
 & & \textit{Never before have I been treated with such incivility! [=rudeness]}\\[0.08cm]
